A redesign was also requested to improve user experience. All marketing assets needed to be available in Japanese and English. I lead a team of six as the lead UX researcher & designer, developer, and project manager. The timeline was 2 months.

User Experience challenge

How do I improve the product inquiry user experience?

KVH has been investing in SEO and PPC efforts to drive traffic to the site to increase contact form inquiries on products and services. Although inquiries have increased, the amount is not as expected and would like to review the user experience of the site.

UX design process

To meet the business goal of increasing inquiries, we first need to look at it as a user experience issue as well, and not strictly a business one. There are many stakeholders in a project, however, the user is notably the focus. Why? Because if the user is happy, the business is happy. You fix the user's issue and it naturally fixes the business issue. Thus, to understand why the inquiries were so low, we need to understand the user and their behavior.

The best place to understand who the users are and what they do is from user data. I interviewed account executives - who work directly with the primary users, analyzed site analytics and user contact form inquiry data, and ran heatmaps of clicks and page scroll. From this I was able to develop personas, use cases, and user scenarios to define the primary users.

With primary users defined and time of the essence, I proceeded with quick and simple usability testing around the office.

Usability issues

Insight 1
Lack of product & service details

Users were unable to find product details and technical specs. This prevented some users from reaching out. They would rather call.

Insight 2
Contact burden of effort and lack of security

The detailed form process prevented users from filling out the form. Some users would rather call instead. SSL/HTTPS was not enabled.

Insight 3
Lack and poor placement of CTAs

Users would exit the site or decide to return later if the contact form wasn't available the moment they were interested.

From the results we gained usability issue insights that allowed us to come up with design recommendations.

Design recommendations

Turn intangible products into tangible where inquiries are just a click away

Recommendation 1
Diagrams & technical specs

Provide technical specifications and diagrams of products & services.

Recommendation 2
Lightweight forms

Create lightweight forms to reduce the burden of effort.

Recommendation 3
Strategic contact CTAs

Contact CTAs used in the right place at the right time.

Scenarios & wireframes

From the multiple possible scenarios, the data showed us the three most trafficked flows with the most issues and obstacles preventing the user from reaching their goal. Wireframes were created for the below scenarios and tested for final insights. Overall, users were happy with the new design and flows. A few complained that the contact form was still too much effort to fill out. We removed the unnecessary fields and details and moved on to high fidelity designs. These high-fidelity designs would be our development reference later for specifications.

Development & technical process

Due two limited team members with development skills, I took the lead on development and technical implementation. An audit of the current site codebase and plugins was conducted to understand the critical and necessary functionality to migrate. Once we understood, what was keep (critical, re-usable) or dump, we were able to reduce the amount of bloat moved to the new site. To rebrand and align with global, we use global styles, UI components and elements. The new rebranded front-end was married with the audited codebase to form a custom theme. With the custom theme in place, we were able to start work on the final UX designs from earlier. Custom functionality was created to meet the design requirements including new UI components and site-wide SSL/HTTPS.

A mobile-first responsive design was also added to address the issue of mobile maintenance, eliminating the need of a separate mobile site. Performance optimization was included as a standard part of building and packaging the code for production. The technical migration part of the project involved DNS change management to handle an array of legacy domains and sub-domains used by various departments. Redirect mapping was also necessary to ensure that key pages and posts were properly redirected to the new site paths. A total of 800+ URIs were mapped to the new site to prevent broken links and loss of SEO backlink work.

Rebranding process

The rebranding process was the most straightforward of the three challenges. The challenge was managing resources; we had a two-person team and needed to create a wide range of collateral in two languages. We were able to bring on board four new temporary staff for the duration of the project who would be able to manage scaling out designs. The content of most collateral didn't need reworking; it was primarily a lift and shift to new branded templates.

We briefed ourselves on the new brand style guide, used the Colt global templates as a base, and began sketching out ways of handling the layout. Once finalized, we began scaling out the designs for all products and services using the newly created templates. All collateral was handled with the same process and many done in parallel, where each member owned a type of collateral. There was little translation work needed and was handled inhouse.
