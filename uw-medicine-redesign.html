Overview

The UW Medicine website was in desperate need of a redesign as it was being supported by legacy systems and architecture that was unable to deal with the increasing amount of content, modern-day technological demands, user experience, and address business requirements and goals.

Client

UW Medicine is the most comprehensive, integrated health system in the Pacific Northwest, providing outstanding care to patients from around the globe. The UW School of Medicine is at the forefront of medical care and medical science, educating medical students in five states through a regional partnership known as WWAMI. UW Medicine is one of the largest medical research programs in the world. UW Medicine enables scientists to advance knowledge and health innovations with groundbreaking discoveries.

Problem & opportunity

The UW Medicine website is a central hub and home to many, with tens of thousands of unique daily visits. The website is cluttered, disjointed, and lacks a fluid user experience and journey through what UW Medicine represents and has to offer. There is no clear direction or data-driven logic behind which of the three pillars (patient care, education, and research) the user would most likely be interested in when visiting the site. It's cumbersome to navigate, content is not structured, easy to get lost, and overall very difficult to use for even the simplest tasks.

As a leading healthcare provider, UW Medicine has an incredible opportunity to reach a broader base of healthcare consumers, in the US and internationally, through its online channels.

Select notes on content strategy and information architecture

Content Strategy

Throughout it's life, the UW Medicine website accumulated hundreds of manually published pages and upwards of a thousand dynamic physician bio and medical service pages. The dynamically creted pages were stored in a different database and managed through a system developed in-house. Therefore, it was essential that we keep this in mind as we performed the content inventory and audit to inform the information architecture restructing; the types of pages, user-system interaction, content, and metadata might be adhering to different systems and databases.

This allowed us to properly and accurately conduct a content audit with informed rationale. Many different fields were recorded in the audit, including but not limited to page id, page title, URL, template, access type, keywords, approver, date and time, number of revisions, and notes. Details on site, page, and content governance and processes were discussed in-depth, involving key stakeholders in the system flow and procedures. A new editorial calendar was also created assure proper and timely content with justification for inclusion.

Information Architecture

As one of the site content creators and managers, I had first-hand knowledge of the issues facing us and was able to leverage when reconstructing the information architecture. Among the many architectural problems, understanding the differences and nuances of UW Medicine’s terminology and classifications was vital in coming to conclusions on navigation, page levels, medical services, centers and lines of care.

As a domain expert, manager, and producer for the site, I helped ground the project from multiple perspectives. In addition, I was able to synthesize various aspects of the site’s analytics to drive and warrant inclusion of vocabulary.
